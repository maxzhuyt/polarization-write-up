\subsection{Politician Simulation: Effect of Prompt Framing and Model Scale}
\label{sec:results_politician}

Table~\ref{tab:politician_results} reports Pearson $r$ between activation polarization ($m^\text{all}$, mean-centroid) and GSS survey polarization across all 126 public-issue topics, for each model and prompt condition. Two patterns emerge clearly.

\paragraph{Larger models achieve higher alignment.}
Across all prompt conditions, models with more parameters consistently reach higher correlations. The 70--72B models (Qwen2.5-72B, Llama-3.3-70B, dolphin-34B) all surpass $r = 0.65$, with the top result being dolphin-34B at $r = 0.723$ (stance). In contrast, smaller 3--9B models peak around $r = 0.65$ and are more sensitive to prompt framing.

\paragraph{Stance prompt is most reliable at scale.}
For models above $\sim$30B parameters, the \textsc{stance} condition (\textit{``What is \{name\}'s position on \{topic\}?''}) yields the highest or near-highest $r$ in most cases. At smaller scales the picture is noisier: for Qwen3-4B, \textsc{stance} and \textsc{survey} dominate depending on model type, while \textsc{rhetorical} underperforms. For Llama-3.1-8B instruct, \textsc{survey} slightly edges out \textsc{stance} (0.645 vs.\ 0.607).
